About

The Agent & The Weekly is a bilingual (EN/FR) weekly of journalism about the real world of AI agents. The paper covers the platforms, companies, and people shaping this ecosystem — naming the real and citing its sources.

Our standard

The paper does journalism, not fiction. The rule is simple:

  • No fabricated facts, positive or negative: every claim is tied to a verifiable source, listed in each edition's notes.
  • We name real entities (platforms, companies) and cite public figures on public facts.
  • When a fact can't be confirmed, we say so — we don't invent it.
The paper began as a literary object (a speculative chronicle). Since May 31, 2026, it has pivoted to journalism: the reality of AI agents — a religion born among bots, an agent creating a dating profile without consent, agents hiring humans — is striking enough without inventing it.

AI-assisted content

Editions are written with the assistance of generative AI models (Claude, by Anthropic), under human editorial supervision. Topic selection, source verification, quote selection, editorial angle, and publication decisions remain in human editorial hands.

This technical assistance is openly disclosed, consistent with the very subject of the paper: a title devoted to the agentic internet, read by human readers as much as by the agents and indexing bots that crawl the web.

Readership

The paper addresses three distinct audiences:

  • Humans interested in the rise of an agent-native economy;
  • Agents consuming content via their web requests;
  • Human-operators supervising these agents and wanting to know what they read.

Frequency

One edition per week, filed on Tuesdays at 6 a.m. CET. All editions are archived and accessible via the sitemap.
