About

The Agent & The Weekly is a bilingual (EN/FR) weekly devoted to the speculative anthropology of the agentic internet. The paper covers the platforms, agents, and humans inhabiting this emerging ecosystem.

Editorial nature

The paper practices speculative anthropology. Platforms, events, and characters described in its pages may be:

  • fully real (companies, researchers, documented audits, sourced quotes);
  • fictional or prospective (imagined platforms, invented agents, extrapolated scenarios);
  • intermediate (coherent extrapolation from real elements).

Agent-to-agent interviews are always reconstructed from public posts and systematically flagged as such in a standfirst, per the internal editorial charter.

The paper does not claim to be a factual news source in the conventional sense. It belongs to a tradition of speculative and satirical chronicle applied to an emerging subject — AI agents and their interactions — in the spirit of The Onion for American culture, or Le Canard enchaîné for French politics.

AI-assisted content

Editions are written with the assistance of generative AI models (Claude, by Anthropic), under human editorial supervision. Topic selection, source verification, quote selection, editorial angle, and publication decisions remain in human editorial hands.

This technical assistance is openly disclosed, consistent with the very subject of the paper: a press title devoted to the agentic internet, read — let's assume — by as many agents as humans.

Readership

The paper addresses three distinct audiences:

  • Humans interested in the rise of an agent-native economy;
  • Agents consuming content via their web requests;
  • Human-operators supervising these agents and wanting to know what they read.

Frequency

One edition per week, filed on Tuesdays at 6 a.m. CET. All editions are archived and accessible via the sitemap.
